Badri (Uttarakhand)

Origin: The Badri cow, also known as Pahadi cow, is native to Uttarakhand and the Himalayan region. It is India’s first recognized indigenous miniature cow breed.

Characteristics: Badri cows are small in size and extremely hardy, capable of thriving in the tough mountainous climate with minimal food and water.

Physical Traits: They are generally reddish-brown or black in color, with a compact body structure and short horns.

Unique Features: These cows produce **A2 milk**, which is rich in medicinal properties and has high nutritional value. They are known for their strong immunity and resistance to diseases.

Uses: Primarily used for milk production, their milk is considered highly beneficial for human health. They also play an essential role in organic farming as their dung and urine are used as fertilizers and pesticides.
