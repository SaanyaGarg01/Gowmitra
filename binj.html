Binjharpuri (Odisha)

Origin: The Binjharpuri breed originates from Odisha, India, and is named after the **Binjharpur region** in Jajpur district. It has been bred and maintained by local farmers for generations.

Characteristics: These cows are well adapted to **hot and humid climates** and are known for their **resistance to diseases** and **low maintenance requirements**. They are **dual-purpose** – used for both **milk production** and **agricultural work**.

Physical Traits: Binjharpuri cows are **medium-sized** with a compact body structure. They usually have a **white or light grey coat**, short horns, and a straight back. They possess **strong legs**, making them ideal for plowing fields.

Unique Features: This breed is known for **producing high-quality A2 milk** with a **good fat percentage**, making it valuable for dairy purposes. The bulls are strong and used for **draft power** in farming.

Uses: Primarily used for **milk production**, their milk has high **nutritional value**. Additionally, **Binjharpuri bulls** are widely used for **plowing fields and other farm work** due to their **strong physique** and **stamina**.
