Tharparkar (Rajasthan)

Origin: The Tharparkar breed originates from the **Thar Desert region**, primarily in **Rajasthan, India**, and **Sindh, Pakistan**.

Characteristics: Tharparkar cattle are **dual-purpose breeds**, valued for both **high milk yield and draught power**. They are well adapted to **harsh desert climates** and can survive on **low-quality fodder**.

Physical Traits: This breed is **medium to large-sized** with a **white or light gray coat**. The cows develop darker shades along the shoulders and hips as they age. Their **hump is well-developed**, and they have **strong, well-built legs**.

Milk Production: Tharparkar cows produce **high-quality A2 milk**, averaging **8-12 liters per day**, with a high **fat percentage (~5%)**, making it ideal for dairy farming.

Uses: Known for their **resilience and endurance**, Tharparkar bulls are used for **agricultural work and cart pulling**. The breed is also valued for **its disease resistance** and ability to thrive in **arid conditions**.
