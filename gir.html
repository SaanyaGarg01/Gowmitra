Gir (Gujarat)

Origin: Gir forest region of Gujarat, India

Characteristics: The Gir breed is known for its high milk production, docile nature, and strong disease resistance. It is one of the best dairy breeds in India and is also exported globally.

Physical Traits: Gir cows have a distinctive convex forehead, long ears, and a reddish-brown coat with white patches. Their skin is loose and glossy, helping them tolerate hot climates.

Unique Features: Gir cows produce A2 milk, which is highly nutritious and beneficial for human health. They are resistant to various tropical diseases and adapt well to different climatic conditions.

Uses: Primarily used for dairy farming due to their high milk yield (average 12-15 liters per day). They are also used for breeding programs worldwide.
