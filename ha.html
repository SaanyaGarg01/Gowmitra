Hariana (Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan)

Origin: The Hariana breed originates from **Haryana**, **Uttar Pradesh**, and **Rajasthan** and is one of the most important **dual-purpose cattle breeds** in India.

Characteristics: Hariana cattle are known for their **hardiness, disease resistance, and excellent adaptability** to different climatic conditions.

Physical Traits: They have a **medium to large-sized frame**, a **white or light gray coat**, and **long, straight, and sharp horns**. Their body is **well-muscled**, making them excellent for both **draught work and milk production**.

Milk Production: Hariana cows produce **moderate amounts of milk** (8-10 liters per day) and are known for their **A2 milk**, which is highly nutritious.
