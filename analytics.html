Indian Cows Analytics

India's cattle population has experienced significant changes over the years, reflecting the nation's evolving agricultural practices and economic development.

Cattle Population Trends

According to the 20th Livestock Census (2019), India's total livestock population reached 536.76 million, marking a 4.6% increase since 2012. The cattle population was recorded at 193.46 million.

Breed Composition

  • Indigenous/Non-descript Cattle: 142.11 million
  • Exotic/Crossbred Cattle: 51.36 million

Milk Production

India is the world's largest milk producer, contributing 24.64% to global milk production. The country's milk production increased from 146.31 million tonnes in 2014-15 to 230.58 million tonnes in 2022-23.

Health and Disease Management

Major livestock diseases reported in 2023:

  • Foot & Mouth Disease (FMD): 38 outbreaks, 159 deaths
  • Haemorrhagic Septicaemia (HS): 152 outbreaks, 54 deaths
  • Black Quarter (BQ): 13 outbreaks, 44 deaths
  • Anthrax: 17 outbreaks, 91 deaths

Regional Distribution

States with the highest cattle populations include Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Madhya Pradesh.
