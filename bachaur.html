Bachaur (Bihar)

Origin: Native to the Bachaur region of Bihar, India, and known for its resilience and adaptability.

Characteristics: Bachaur cows are small to medium-sized, hardy, and well-adapted to the local climate and terrain. They are mainly used for both milk production and agricultural work.

Physical Traits: These cows have a white or grey coat with a well-developed muscular body. Their horns are medium-sized and slightly curved.

Unique Features: They are known for their ability to survive on minimal feed and withstand extreme climatic conditions, making them a low-maintenance breed.

Uses: Used for milk production as well as draft purposes in farming, making them highly versatile.
