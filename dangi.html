Dangi (Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh)

Origin: Western Ghats, Maharashtra, and parts of Madhya Pradesh, India

Characteristics: Dangi cattle are highly valued for their endurance and adaptability, especially in heavy rainfall areas. They are primarily used as draught animals for agricultural work.

Physical Traits: This breed has a medium-sized body with a deep, compact structure. The coat color varies from red, black, and white to brown with white patches. Their skin secretes oil, which makes them resistant to excessive rain and humidity.

Unique Features: Dangi cattle have excellent resistance to tropical diseases and can work efficiently even in extreme weather conditions. Their sweat glands help in better thermoregulation.

Uses: Primarily used for plowing, cart-pulling, and other heavy-duty tasks in farming. Milk production is moderate, but the breed is mostly prized for its labor capacity.
