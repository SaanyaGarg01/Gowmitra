Cutchi (Kutch, Gujarat)

Origin: The Cutchi breed, also known as **Kankrej**, originates from the **Kutch and Banaskantha regions of Gujarat**.

Characteristics: This breed is **well-adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions** and is highly **resistant to diseases**. It is known for **its endurance, strength, and ability to survive in harsh environments**.

Physical Traits: Cutchi cows are **large-sized with a compact, muscular build**. They have a **silver-gray to iron-gray coat**, large **lyre-shaped horns**, and a **well-developed hump**.

Milk Production: The breed is a **dual-purpose breed**, used for both **milk production and draught work**. Cutchi cows produce **8-12 liters of A2 milk per day**, which has a **high-fat content (~5%)**, making it valuable for dairy farming.

Uses: Cutchi bulls are used for **plowing fields, pulling carts, and other heavy work** due to their **strength and stamina**. Their ability to **thrive in dry climates** makes them a preferred choice for farmers in **desert and drought-prone regions**.
