Amrit Mahal (Karnataka)

Origin: Karnataka, India

Characteristics: Amrit Mahal is one of the finest draught cattle breeds in India, known for its speed, endurance, and strength. It is primarily used for agricultural work, transportation, and military purposes due to its sturdy build.

Physical Traits: Amrit Mahal cattle have a compact, muscular body with a deep chest, well-developed limbs, and sharp horns that curve slightly upward. Their coat is usually gray or white.

Unique Features: This breed is highly resistant to harsh climatic conditions and can survive with minimal resources. Historically, it was used by Indian armies for carrying heavy loads.

Uses: Primarily used for plowing fields, pulling carts, and other agricultural tasks. It is not a high milk-producing breed but excels in draught power.
