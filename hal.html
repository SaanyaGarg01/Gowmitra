Hallikar (Karnataka)

Origin: The Hallikar breed originates from the **Karnataka** region of India and is one of the oldest and most well-known draught cattle breeds.

Characteristics: Hallikar cattle are known for their **extraordinary strength, endurance, and speed**, making them highly preferred for agricultural purposes, particularly **plowing and cart-pulling**.

Physical Traits: These cows have a **medium to large-sized body**, with a **muscular build**. Their coat is typically **white or grey**, with **sharp, long, and backward-curving horns**.

Unique Features: Hallikar bulls are widely regarded as some of the **best draft animals in India**, and they have played a key role in developing **other cattle breeds** such as **Amrit Mahal**.

Uses: Primarily used for **agricultural activities**, these cattle are **extremely hardy** and can work in **hot and rough terrains** for long hours. Although not primarily a dairy breed, Hallikar cows **produce A2 milk**, which is valued for its nutritional benefits.
